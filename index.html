15 Popular Filipino Recipes

The Philippines’ cuisine is a vibrant mix of flavors and traditions, featuring savory classics like ,Adobo,Sinigang , and Kare-Kare, festive highlights such as Lechon and Pancit, and comforting soups like Tinola and Bulalo. Regional specialties like Bicol Express and Laing add spice, while street food favorites like Sisig and Lumpiang Shanghaibring bold tastes. Sweet endings are celebrated with Halo-Halo and Bibingka, showcasing the country's rich culinary diversity and festive spirit .

  • Adobo

    A savory and tangy dish made by simmering chicken or pork in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices.

  • Sinigang

    A sour and comforting tamarind-based soup, often cooked with pork, shrimp, or fish and assorted vegetables.

  • Lechon

    A whole roasted pig with crispy skin and juicy meat, often served during celebrations.

  • Kare-Kare

    A creamy peanut-based stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables, paired with bagoong (shrimp paste).

  • Pancit

    Stir-fried noodles mixed with chicken, shrimp, vegetables, and a squeeze of calamansi, symbolizing long life.

  • Sisig

    A sizzling dish of chopped pork face, liver, and onions, seasoned with calamansi and chilies.

  • Tinola

    A clear chicken soup with ginger, green papaya, and malunggay leaves for a light and flavorful meal.

  • Laing

    Dried taro leaves cooked in rich coconut milk with shrimp and chilies for a spicy, creamy taste.

  • Bicol Express

    A hearty beef shank soup with bone marrow, corn, and vegetables, perfect for cold days.

  • Lumpiang Shanghai

    Crispy fried spring rolls filled with seasoned ground pork and vegetables, often served as an appetizer.

  • Tapsilog

    A classic Filipino breakfast of cured beef (tapa), garlic fried rice, and a fried egg.

  • Halo-Halo

    A colorful shaved ice dessert layered with sweet beans, fruits, leche flan, and ube.

  • Bibingka

    A soft rice cake baked in banana leaves, topped with salted egg and grated coconut.

  • Dinuguan

    A savory stew of pork and pig’s blood simmered with vinegar, garlic, and spices.
